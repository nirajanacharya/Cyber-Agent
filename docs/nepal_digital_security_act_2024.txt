NEPAL'S DIGITAL SECURITY ACT, 2024

This is Nepal's comprehensive cybersecurity legislation addressing modern digital threats, data protection, and cyber incident response.

OVERVIEW:

The Digital Security Act, 2024 modernizes Nepal's cybersecurity framework to address evolving threats including:
- Ransomware attacks and data breaches
- Cloud security and data protection
- IoT security and smart device vulnerabilities
- Critical infrastructure protection
- Personal data protection and privacy rights
- AI and machine learning security
- Incident response and breach reporting

CHAPTER 1: DEFINITIONS AND SCOPE

Article 1: Applicability
- Applies to all digital systems and networks in Nepal
- Covers both government and private sector organizations
- Includes individuals using digital services
- Applies to Nepal-based organizations and international organizations serving Nepal

Article 2: Key Definitions
- Digital Security: Protection of information and systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction
- Cyber Incident: Unauthorized access, data breach, malware infection, or system disruption
- Critical Information Infrastructure: Systems essential to national security, economy, or public services
- Personal Data: Information related to identified or identifiable individuals
- Data Controller: Organization determining purposes and means of data processing
- Data Processor: Organization processing data on behalf of controller

CHAPTER 2: DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY

Article 3: Data Protection Rights
- Every individual has right to privacy and data protection
- Personal data collection must be lawful and transparent
- Data must be used only for stated purposes
- Individuals have right to access their personal data
- Right to correction, deletion, and portability of data

Article 4: Consent Requirements
- Explicit consent required before collecting personal data
- Consent must be freely given, specific, and informed
- Cannot collect personal data without consent (limited exceptions for government)
- Children's data requires parental consent
- Sensitive data (health, financial, biometric) requires higher protection

Article 5: Data Minimization
- Only collect data actually needed for stated purpose
- Do not collect excessive personal information
- Delete data when no longer needed
- Implement data retention schedules
- Cannot share data with third parties without consent

Article 6: Security Standards
- Implement technical and organizational security measures
- Protect data with encryption and access controls
- Conduct regular security audits and penetration testing
- Maintain incident response plans
- Implement backup and disaster recovery procedures
- Use certified security products and services

Article 7: Data Breach Notification
- Organizations must report data breaches within 72 hours to authority
- Notify affected individuals promptly
- Inform law enforcement and security agencies
- Provide details of breach scope and remediation measures
- Public disclosure for breaches affecting many individuals

CHAPTER 3: CYBERSECURITY REQUIREMENTS

Article 8: Mandatory Security Measures for Organizations
- Implement firewalls and intrusion detection systems
- Deploy antivirus and anti-malware protection
- Maintain secure coding practices in software development
- Implement access controls and authentication mechanisms
- Conduct regular security training for employees
- Maintain security incident logs and monitoring
- Implement incident response and disaster recovery plans

Article 9: Minimum Technical Standards
- Use encryption for sensitive data (minimum 256-bit for critical systems)
- Implement multi-factor authentication for administrative access
- Regular security patching and vulnerability management
- Network segmentation and isolation of critical systems
- Secure configuration of systems and applications
- Removal of default credentials and unnecessary services

Article 10: Third-Party Security
- Verify security of vendors and service providers
- Include security requirements in contracts
- Regular audits of third-party systems
- Data protection agreements required
- Liability for third-party security failures
- Right to inspect and audit third-party systems

CHAPTER 4: CYBERCRIME OFFENSES AND PENALTIES

Article 11: Unauthorized Access
Offense: Attempting to access computer systems without authorization
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 200,000 and/or imprisonment up to 2 years
Aggravated (causing damage): Fine up to NPR 500,000 and/or imprisonment up to 5 years

Article 12: Malware Distribution
Offense: Creating, distributing, or using viruses, worms, ransomware
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 300,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years
If causing damage/loss: Fine up to NPR 1,000,000 and/or imprisonment up to 10 years

Article 13: Data Breaches and Theft
Offense: Stealing, copying, or unauthorized access to personal or confidential data
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 500,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years
Enhanced penalty for sensitive data (health, financial, biometric)

Article 14: Ransomware Attacks
Offense: Deploying ransomware or demanding ransom for system restoration
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 1,000,000 and/or imprisonment up to 5 years
If threatening national security: Up to 10 years imprisonment

Article 15: Financial Cybercrime
Offense: Online banking fraud, payment manipulation, cryptocurrency theft
Penalty: Fine up to 2x the amount stolen + imprisonment
Maximum 10 years for large-scale fraud

Article 16: Identity Theft and Fraud
Offense: Using someone's identity or credentials without permission
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 300,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years
Plus liability for damages caused

Article 17: Phishing and Social Engineering
Offense: Creating fraudulent websites, phishing emails to steal credentials
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 200,000 and/or imprisonment up to 2 years
Enhanced penalty if targeting government or critical infrastructure

Article 18: Denial of Service Attacks
Offense: DDoS attacks disrupting system availability
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 500,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years
If disrupting critical infrastructure: Up to 10 years imprisonment

Article 19: Cyber Harassment and Threats
Offense: Online harassment, cyberbullying, threats, defamation
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 100,000 and/or imprisonment up to 1 year
Enhanced penalty for threats to safety or security

Article 20: Non-Consensual Intimate Images
Offense: Sharing intimate images without consent
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 500,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years
Victim can seek damages and right to erasure

CHAPTER 5: CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION

Article 21: Critical Infrastructure Definition
- Power generation and distribution systems
- Water supply and management systems
- Transportation networks (air, rail, road)
- Telecommunications and internet infrastructure
- Healthcare systems and hospitals
- Financial systems and banks
- Government administrative systems
- Emergency services systems

Article 22: Critical Infrastructure Security Requirements
- Implement air-gapped networks for critical systems
- Use hardware security modules for key management
- Conduct regular penetration testing and security audits
- Implement 24/7 monitoring and incident response
- Maintain redundancy and failover systems
- Regular security training for operators
- Compliance with international cybersecurity standards

Article 23: Attack on Critical Infrastructure
Offense: Any cyber attack on critical infrastructure
Penalty: Fine up to NPR 2,000,000 and/or imprisonment up to 15 years
If causing death/injury: Up to life imprisonment or death penalty

CHAPTER 6: COMPLIANCE AND GOVERNANCE

Article 24: Cybersecurity Authority
- Nepal to establish Cybersecurity Authority (or through designated agency)
- Responsibility for policy, standards, and enforcement
- Coordination with international cybersecurity bodies
- Technical assistance and capacity building

Article 25: Mandatory Incident Reporting
- Critical infrastructure operators must report attacks within 24 hours
- Other organizations must report within 72 hours
- Include incident details, scope, and remediation
- Cooperation with law enforcement investigations
- False reporting is punishable offense

Article 26: Security Audit and Compliance
- Regular audits required for critical infrastructure (annually)
- Large organizations must conduct annual security assessments
- Third-party auditors must be certified
- Audit reports submitted to Cybersecurity Authority
- Non-compliance results in fines and penalties

Article 27: Government Systems
- All government systems must comply with Digital Security Act
- Enhanced security for classified and sensitive information
- Regular testing and disaster recovery drills
- Security training for all government employees
- Designated Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

CHAPTER 7: INVESTIGATION AND ENFORCEMENT

Article 28: Investigation Authority
- Nepal Police has authority to investigate cybercrimes
- Court warrants required for system access and data seizure
- Digital evidence must follow chain of custody procedures
- International cooperation for cross-border investigations

Article 29: Law Enforcement Access
- Emergency access to systems without warrant in critical situations
- Post-event judicial review within 24 hours
- ISPs must preserve logs for 1 year minimum
- Cooperation with law enforcement is mandatory

Article 30: Penalties and Fines
- Summary: NPR 100,000 - NPR 2,000,000 depending on offense
- Imprisonment: 1 - 15 years depending on severity
- Confiscation of equipment and proceeds of crime
- Victim compensation for damages

CHAPTER 8: INTERNATIONAL COOPERATION

Article 31: Cross-Border Investigations
- Nepal cooperates with international cybersecurity bodies
- Mutual legal assistance agreements with other countries
- Joint task forces for major cybercrimes
- Information sharing with international partners
- Harmonization with international cybersecurity standards

PRACTICAL GUIDANCE FOR USERS:

1. PERSONAL CYBERSECURITY:
   - Use strong, unique passwords for each account
   - Enable two-factor authentication on important accounts
   - Keep devices updated with latest security patches
   - Use reputable antivirus and anti-malware software
   - Be cautious of phishing emails and suspicious links
   - Do not share personal information unnecessarily
   - Use VPN for secure browsing in public networks
   - Backup important data regularly

2. ORGANIZATIONAL CYBERSECURITY:
   - Designate a cybersecurity officer
   - Implement security awareness training for all employees
   - Maintain security incident logs and monitoring
   - Conduct regular security audits and assessments
   - Implement access controls and authentication
   - Encrypt sensitive data
   - Maintain incident response and disaster recovery plans
   - Establish data protection policies
   - Vet third-party vendors and service providers

3. INCIDENT RESPONSE:
   - If compromised: Disconnect from network immediately
   - Change all passwords from secure device
   - Report to cybersecurity authority within 72 hours
   - Preserve evidence for law enforcement
   - Notify affected individuals and stakeholders
   - Seek professional cybersecurity assistance
   - Consider cyber insurance coverage

4. REPORTING CYBERCRIMES:
   - Contact Nepal Police Cyber Bureau
   - File complaints with appropriate authorities
   - Provide detailed incident information and evidence
   - Cooperate with law enforcement investigations
   - Seek legal counsel for civil remedies

5. LEGAL RIGHTS:
   - Right to privacy and data protection
   - Right to access personal data held by organizations
   - Right to correction and deletion of inaccurate data
   - Right to know how data is being used
   - Right to compensation for data breaches
   - Right to report cybercrime to authorities
   - Right to legal action against perpetrators
