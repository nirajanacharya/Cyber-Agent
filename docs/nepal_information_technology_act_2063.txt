NEPAL'S INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY ACT, 2063 (2006)

This Act establishes the legal framework for information technology, electronic transactions, and cybersecurity in Nepal.

KEY PROVISIONS:

Section 1: Scope and Application
- Applies to the use of information technology in Nepal
- Covers electronic records and digital signatures
- Establishes framework for cybercrime and penalties

Section 2: Definition of Terms
- Information Technology: Technology relating to computer systems, networks, and digital data
- Cybercrime: Illegal activities conducted using computer systems or networks
- Electronic Record: Data stored, generated, or transmitted in digital form
- Digital Signature: Electronic signature meeting prescribed standards

Section 3: Validity of Electronic Records
- Electronic records are recognized as legally valid evidence
- Digital signatures are equivalent to handwritten signatures when properly applied
- Electronic transactions have same legal validity as paper-based transactions

Section 4: Unauthorized Access to Computer Systems
- Illegal access to computer systems is prohibited
- Penalty: Fine up to NPR 10,000 and/or imprisonment up to 1 year
- Accessing protected systems without authorization is cybercrime

Section 5: Data Theft and Unauthorized Copying
- Stealing or copying data from computer systems is prohibited
- Includes intellectual property and confidential information theft
- Penalty: Fine up to NPR 100,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years

Section 6: Computer Fraud
- Using computers to commit fraud is prohibited
- Includes manipulation of data, false records, and deceptive transactions
- Penalty: Fine up to NPR 50,000 and/or imprisonment up to 2 years

Section 7: Malware and Harmful Software
- Creating, distributing, or using viruses, worms, and malware is prohibited
- Includes ransomware and other malicious code
- Penalty: Fine up to NPR 100,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years

Section 8: Hacking and System Disruption
- Unauthorized interference with computer systems is prohibited
- Includes network disruption, DDoS attacks, and system tampering
- Penalty: Fine up to NPR 100,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years

Section 9: Obscene Content Online
- Distributing or possessing obscene content is prohibited
- Includes child exploitation material (CSAM)
- Penalty: Fine up to NPR 100,000 and/or imprisonment up to 5 years

Section 10: Privacy and Data Protection
- Unauthorized access to private communications is prohibited
- Email interception and wiretapping are illegal
- Penalty: Fine up to NPR 50,000 and/or imprisonment up to 2 years

Section 11: Digital Signature Standards
- Digital signatures must meet prescribed technical standards
- Use of certified digital signatures is legally valid
- Signature verification through certified authorities is required

Section 12: Electronic Document Retention
- Electronic documents must be retained for legal compliance
- Long-term archiving standards must be followed
- Electronic records serve as valid legal evidence

OFFENSE CATEGORIES:

1. Critical Infrastructure Attacks (Highest Severity)
   - Attacking government or essential services systems
   - Penalty: Fine up to NPR 500,000 and/or imprisonment up to 10 years

2. Cyber Terrorism and National Security
   - Acts threatening national security through cyber means
   - Coordinated attacks on critical systems
   - Penalty: Up to life imprisonment

3. Financial Cybercrime
   - Online banking fraud and transaction manipulation
   - Digital payment system fraud
   - Penalty: Fine equal to 3x fraud amount + imprisonment

4. Privacy Violations
   - Unauthorized access to personal data
   - Misuse of private information
   - Penalty: Fine up to NPR 100,000 + imprisonment up to 3 years

INVESTIGATIVE POWERS:

- Police can investigate cybercrime with technical expertise
- Court orders required for accessing encrypted data
- ISPs and telecom companies must preserve logs for authorities
- International cooperation for cross-border cybercrimes

LEGAL PROTECTIONS:

- Users have right to secure digital transactions
- Data protection and privacy rights recognized
- Victims of cybercrime can file complaints
- Compensation available for cybercrime victims
- Confidentiality of investigation sources protected

IMPORTANT NOTES FOR USERS:

1. Always use strong passwords and two-factor authentication
2. Do not share personal information online unnecessarily
3. Be cautious of phishing emails and suspicious links
4. Keep software and systems updated with latest security patches
5. Do not attempt to access systems without authorization
6. Report cybercrime to Nepal Police Cyber Bureau
7. Use licensed cybersecurity services for business systems
8. Maintain backup copies of important data
9. Use VPN for secure browsing in public networks
10. Never attempt to help hackers or provide hacking services
